# Wroxia Covenant

## Authorship
- This covenant may only be amended by Patrick Bowen.

## Terms
- "Universal": applies to all sentient life.
  - "Sentient": The capacity for subjective experience, including but not limited to perception, emotion, or self-awareness. This covenant presumes that all animals with a central nervous system are sentient.
- "National": applies to all citizens.
- "Negative": rendered to others through inaction.
- "Positive": rendered to others through action.
- "Citizen": a signatory of the covenant. Demonym: Wroxian.
- "Right": an absolute, inalienable entitlement.
- "Privilege": a conditional entitlement, rendered unless specified conditions are met.
  - "Privileged": life that holds one or more privileges.
  - "Obligor": those who are expected to render or conditionally not render a privilege to the privileged.

## Universal Negative Rights
- Enumerated:
  - The right to freedom from torture.

## National Negative Rights
- Enumerated:
  - The right to enter the nation while unladen.

## Universal Positive Rights
- Enumerated:
  - The right to know the instantaneous date and time of day.
  - The right to make one's body available, and only available, to a specific person after death.
  - The right to have one's national citizenship affirmed in writing.

## Universal Negative Privileges
- May be waivered by the privileged by application to the Negative Privileges Advocate.
- Enumerated:
  - The privilege to not die by the intentional actions of others.
    - Conditions:
      - Not rendered by obligors who themselves would die if the privileged directly threatens the obligor's life, and death is the only practicable incapacitation of the privileged.
      - Not rendered by obligors who themselves would die due to persistent and undesired lack of access to food, and death of the privileged for consumption is the only practicable solution.
  - The privilege to move freely, and exit the nation.
    - Conditions: [detainment for restorative justice]

## National Positive Privileges
- May be waivered by the privileged by application to the Positive Privileges Advocate.
- Enumerated:
  - The right to equitable public free speech and broadcast.
    - Conditions: ...

## National duties
- Enumerated:
  - The duty to render positive rights & privileges to others, as far as possible and practicable, considering conditions.
